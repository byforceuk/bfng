# BFNG copy audit and patch notes

## Main copy problems found

1. The home page was partly written for the seller, not the trade owner. Phrases such as “benchmark package,” “before the pitch,” “what the buyer sees,” and “outreach-led sales” made the page feel like internal sales strategy rather than customer-facing SaaS copy.

2. The offer had pricing conflict. Trade-specific pages still mentioned “£297 setup + £97/mo,” while the main site positioned the core install from £995 + £149/mo and the flagship from £1,495 + £199/mo.

3. Trade pages used generic copy across multiple sectors. Several pages said the customer may be standing next to a “fault, leak, lockout, blocked drain or project problem,” which made plumbers, electricians, locksmiths, builders and drainage pages feel spun rather than trade-specific.

4. Some claims were too broad or soft. “Recover missed jobs” and “growth promise” style wording was tightened into “capture,” “track,” “follow up,” and “reduce leakage” language to avoid overpromising outcomes.

5. The site underused the strongest buying logic: a trade owner does not need another dashboard; they need serious enquiries kept alive until someone calls back, quotes, books or closes them.

## What was patched

- Rewrote the hero headline and lead paragraph to make the value proposition faster and more direct.
- Replaced internal-facing sales language with buyer-facing copy.
- Reframed the flagship package around high-intent enquiry capture, not “4-figure positioning.”
- Made the audit section sound like a diagnostic value step rather than a sales tactic.
- Reworked proof/visibility sections so the customer sees what they get before committing.
- Updated pricing section copy to align with the installed-package model.
- Rewrote trade-specific pages for plumbers, electricians, roofers, locksmiths, drainage companies, and builders/landscapers.
- Removed old £297/£97 pricing references from trade pages.
- Updated footers, nav CTA wording, privacy/terms phrasing and the AI comparison page for consistency.

## Positioning now

BFNG is positioned as an installed enquiry recovery system for UK trade businesses: missed-call capture, quote/callback forms, lead tracking, follow-up reminders, reporting and upgrade path to AI/CRM later.
